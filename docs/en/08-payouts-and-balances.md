# Payouts & Balances

This applies equally to both earner types the platform tracks commission for: **partners** (referral commission) and **commission-based employees** (service commission).

## How an earner's balance is calculated

At any moment, an earner's financial position is made up of three numbers:

- **Earned** — the total commission they've accumulated, across everything they've earned commission on.
- **Paid** — the total that has already actually been paid out to them.
- **Reserved** — commission that's been credited but is still going through the review timing described in the relevant earning source (for example, the return-window period on a referred shop order).

Their genuinely **available** balance — what they can actually request or be paid right now — is their total earned, minus what's already been paid, minus whatever is still reserved. This calculation is always based on real, current figures rather than a running tally that could drift out of sync, so it accurately reflects the true state of their account at the moment it's checked, including anything paid out since the last time it was looked at.

## Requesting a payout

An earner can submit a request for any amount up to their currently available balance. The system won't accept a request for more than that, so there's no way to request money that isn't genuinely available yet.

## How a request gets resolved

An administrator reviewing a payout request can:

- **Approve** it, marking it as accepted and in progress.
- **Mark it paid**, once the money has actually been sent.
- **Reject** it, with an explanatory note the earner can see.

Separately, an administrator can also **record a payout directly** — for situations where money changed hands outside the normal request flow (a cash payment made in person, for instance) and simply needs to be reflected in the system afterward, without the earner needing to have submitted a request for it first.

Whichever path is used, the earner is notified by email the moment their payout's status changes, including the reason if a request is declined — so they're never left wondering what happened to a request they submitted.
